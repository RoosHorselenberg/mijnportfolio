Product comparer is an application where users can compare different products from Albert Heijn and get personalized suggestions based on their own goals. By giving them the facts about the product we want to make the customer more conscious of there decision and chose a product that is best for their health. By providing an overview of product differences and inspiring customers to make healthier choices, Albert Heijn hopes to assist their customers in making decisions about what to buy. It takes a lot of time and effort to compare products using the app or the website. The aim is to change that by developing an interactive module that can be used to make conscious product choices.

Task

As the UX designer for this project, my task was to create a seamless and engaging user experience for the Product Comparer application. I focused on designing an interactive module that would make it easy for users to compare products and receive personalized recommendations tailored to their health goals. My role involved understanding the challenges users faced when comparing products and developing a design that would simplify this process, helping users make informed choices more efficiently.

Research

For research, we first conducted a survey to identify user needs. What do people compare products on most? For example, price, ingredients, nutritional values or origin? Which nutritional values do people find most important? And how important do people think it is that the products they buy are healthy? Besides the preliminary research we conducted, usability tests were also carried out with Albert Heijn customers. Based on the results, fitting iterations were made.

User flow

I set up a user flow for this project because I wanted to ensure that users could seamlessly navigate through the experience, understanding each step intuitively. By organizing the content and interactions in a logical sequence, I made sure that users could achieve their goals without confusion or frustration.
